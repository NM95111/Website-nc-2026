Tips for First-Timers in Lebanon

Coming to Lebanon for the first time? You're in for a treat. Here's everything you need to know before you arrive.

✈️

MEA & Transavia offer direct flights from Europe including London, Paris and Amsterdam

🛂

Your passport must be valid for at least 3 months beyond your entry date and have at least 2 blank pages. No Israeli stamps, visas, or seals.

📞

When you arrive, you may be asked for: return/onward ticket, proof of accommodation, and a contact number in Lebanon — use Yara Boulos: +96170120801

📱

Get an E-SIM installed before you travel, or pick up a SIM at the airport with internet bundles

💵

Cards are accepted in most places, but keep some cash on you in USD or LBP

🚕

Taxis are only payable by cash

🚗

Car rentals are available at the airport, but buckle up — driving in Lebanon is not for the faint-hearted

🚰

Please don't drink the tap water as it is not filtered

🍽️

Lebanese dishes not to miss: mannousheh cheese & zaatar, kebbeh, fatteh, weras anab

🗣️

English & French are widely spoken — feel free to show off your Arabic habibi!

📱

Toters is the local delivery app to satisfy any midnight cravings!

🗺️

Please make sure not to go to Dahiye and the South

🚨

Emergency: Red Cross 140  ·  Police 112

Things to Do in Lebanon

Lebanon is a country like no other — ancient history, stunning nature, and a vibrant food and nightlife scene all in one place. Here are our top picks to make the most of your stay:

Pigeon Rocks (Raouché)

The most iconic landmark in Beirut — two natural rock arches rising from the sea at Raouché. Best at sunset.

Jeita Grotto

A spectacular system of limestone caves 18km north of Beirut, considered one of the natural wonders of the world.

Baalbek

Home to some of the best-preserved Roman ruins in the world, including the magnificent Temple of Jupiter.

Byblos (Jbeil)

One of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. Explore the ancient port, crusader castle, and charming old souk.

Beirut Corniche

A beloved 4.8km seafront promenade — great for a morning walk, sunset stroll, or watching local life unfold.

Cedars of God (Bsharri)

A UNESCO World Heritage Site — a sacred grove of ancient cedar trees in the mountains of northern Lebanon.

Faraya & Mzaar (Skiing)

Ski in Lebanon! Faraya Mzaar is the largest ski resort in the Middle East, just 1.5 hours from Beirut.

Qadisha Valley

A UNESCO-listed valley with ancient monasteries carved into dramatic cliffsides — one of Lebanon's most beautiful hikes.

Tyre (Sour)

A UNESCO World Heritage Site with Roman hippodrome ruins, ancient columns, and beautiful Mediterranean beaches.

Zaitunay Bay

Beirut's chic waterfront marina lined with restaurants, cafés, and stunning views of the city skyline.

Tripoli

Lebanon's second city is home to a magnificent crusader citadel, bustling souks, and the best kanafeh in the country.

Chouf & Beiteddine Palace

A stunning 19th-century Lebanese palace set amid the Chouf mountains, surrounded by cedar and oak forests.
